The Expansion of the Universe

From the Big Bang to Today

The Big Bang:
The universe began about 13.8 billion years ago from an extremely hot and dense state known as the Big Bang. Space itself started expanding, carrying matter and energy with it.
Galaxies Moving Apart:
Edwin Hubble discovered that galaxies are moving away from us, and the farther they are, the faster they move. This means the universe is expanding everywhere, not from a central point.
Accelerating Expansion:
Observations of distant supernovae show that the expansion of the universe is speeding up, likely due to a mysterious force called dark energy.
Dark Energy:
Dark energy makes up about 68% of the universe and is responsible for the accelerated expansion. The rest is dark matter and ordinary matter.
