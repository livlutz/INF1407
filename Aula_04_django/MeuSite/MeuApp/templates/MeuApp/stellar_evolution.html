The life of the star is based on the balance of gravitational (due to its mass) and radiation pressure (from fusion on its core).

It goes as follows: Nebula -> protostar -> main sequence -> Red giant -> white dwarf or Red supergiant -> supernova -> neutron stars or balck holes

I intend to add more information about each stage in the future, but here is a brief overview

Nebula
Region of dust and particles where matter clumps together under gravitational attraction.
Protostar
A star that is able to fuse Hydrogen (H2).
Main Sequence
Gravitational force is now balanced by Hydrogen fusion. Also considered a protostar that could sustain Hydrogen fusion. The star spends most of its life in this stage.
Red Giant & supergiant
Hydrogen in the core runs out, so there is only Helium (He) left to burn. Radiation pressure drops and the core contracts, becoming hotter, fusing heavier elements. Its outer layers expand, so now the star has a larger surface area. Therefore it is more powerful and brighter, but as the power is more spread out, its temperature decreases and its wavelength increases, making appears red. Stars over approximately 8 solar masses (High mass main sequence star) become red supergiants, while stars below this limit (Low mass mains sequence stars) become red giants.
High mass main sequence
Forms a red supergiant after using up its core Hydrogen. When it runs out of fuel, it burns heavier elements to balance gravity, but it can only do so up to Iron (Fe) as it has the highest binding energy (Energy required to separate atomic components) per nucleon. As it burns heavier elements, it becomes unstable and starts to absoreb energy instead o emitting. Pressure decreases, causing the core to collapse and an expansion of the outer layers, creating a supernova.
Low mass main sequence
Forms a red giant after using up its core Hydrogen. It cannot burn heavier elements, so it contracts and the outer layers are ejected, leaving behind a planetary nebula and a white dwarf.
Supernova
Ejects all elements fused in the star's core to the universe. If the remaining core is > 1.4 solar masses (Chandrasekhar limit), it becomes a neutron star or, if it is > 3 solar masses, a black hole can be formed if it is < 2GM/cÂ² (schwarzchild radius)*.
Black hole
A black hole can be formed if the Red supergiant's core is < schwarzchild radius. I will add more information about black holes in the future.
White dwarfs
The core of a red giant star, which is now very hot and dim. Eventually, a white dwarf reaches a point where its mass will no longer be able to fuse heavier elements, so it will become a brown dward and eventually a black dwarf, even more dim.

*Schwarzchild radius: The radius below which the escape velocity exceeds the speed of light, meaning not even light can escape its gravitational pull.
